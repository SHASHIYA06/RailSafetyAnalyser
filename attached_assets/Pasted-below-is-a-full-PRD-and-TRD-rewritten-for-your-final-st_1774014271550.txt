The attached document makes the product goal clear: build a full railway and metro RAMS application where a user can search any electrical, mechanical, or electronic component and get applicable standards, safety/reliability details, study materials, uploaded documents, and searchable results from indexed RAMS content. It also asks for standards search, document upload with chunking and indexing, Google Drive document access, voice-based search, exports, and a daily 5 PM sync for rail and metro news.

This architecture directly supports the attached requirements for search, uploads, Drive integration, voice search, AI workflows, exports, and scheduled refresh jobs.

Architecture

The application should use a hybrid structured + semantic retrieval model because your attached requirements include both exact lookup of standards/components and AI-assisted document search over uploaded RAMS materials. Structured data lives in Neon, raw files live in Firebase Storage, extracted text chunks and embeddings are written to Neon, and Firebase Functions provide search, ingestion, sync, and AI answer APIs.
​

Core flow

Vercel frontend calls Firebase HTTPS endpoints.

Firebase verifies Firebase Auth token.

API reads/writes structured records in Neon.

Uploaded files go to Firebase Storage.

Ingestion workers extract text, chunk it, generate embeddings, and store chunks in Neon.

Retrieval service combines keyword filters, SQL relations, and vector similarity.

LLM service uses OpenRouter or Gemini to answer with citations.

Scheduler runs daily news/document refresh at 5 PM.

AI design

Use Gemini for multimodal-heavy tasks like long-document extraction, document summarization, and optional image/PDF reasoning, and use OpenRouter for model routing, fallback, and cost/performance control across answer-generation models. This setup fits the attached request for special AI tools, automation, document understanding, and smart search across uploaded materials.

Important constraints
Your attached document asks for “all railway standards” and full standards-based search, but in production the system should not store or display copyrighted standards text unless you own the documents or have distribution rights. The correct production approach is to store metadata, clause references, your licensed/internal documents, user-uploaded files, and AI-generated summaries/snippets derived from permitted content.
